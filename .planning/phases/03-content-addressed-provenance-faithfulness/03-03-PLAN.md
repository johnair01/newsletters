<objective>
Enforce faithfulness as deterministic span-containment at the Phase-1 socket seam (PROV-02,
success criteria 2 and 3; decisions D-3 and D-4). Add a stdlib-only `SpanContainmentFaithfulness`
checker — normalize (collapse whitespace + case-fold), then require the claim text to be contained
in one of its traced evidence spans — implementing the existing `FaithfulnessCheck` protocol
(`entails(claim) -> bool`). Make it the DEFAULT check at the single-place `_enforce` seam (and
therefore at `assert_conforms`), so every backend inherits it with zero backend change. Add a
route-to-missing helper so a claim that cannot be located in/entailed by its own trace is moved to
`Distillation.missing[]`, never surfaced as a fact.

Purpose: PROV-02 makes "audited" structurally honest — an unfaithful claim cannot pass as a fact.
Enforcing at the seam (not per-backend) means the manual backend today and every adapter/AI backend
later inherit the gate for free (D-3: enforced at the socket seam so all backends inherit it). The
gate is FAITHFUL, NOT SUGGESTIVE: it only checks containment and relocates the unfaithful claim to
missing[] — it never rewrites a claim or edits a span. AI entailment is explicitly OUT of scope
(D-4): the deterministic no-AI checker only.

Output: `distill/faithfulness.py` (the checker + the route-to-missing helper), `_enforce`/
`assert_conforms` defaulted to span-containment, the new symbols re-exported, and a RED-first
`tests/test_faithfulness_gate.py`.
</objective>
